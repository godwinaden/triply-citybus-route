About Hartberg

The City of Hartberg (Styria) in rural Austria currently has a rather lackluster public transport coverage. In detail, they have a local Citybus , regular Postbus public transport buses and a local train station. However, they currently experience a minor population influx and know that in the near future, their population will increase drastically (about 10% increase).

They would like to improve their current public transport network ahead of time, in order to allow new residents to commute to their workplaces using sustainable transport right from the start. A working group in the City government has now proposed a change of schedule forf the existing Citybus (from once an hour from 9:00 until 16:00 to twice per hour between 7:00 and 18:00).

The working group thinks this will make the Citybus more attractive to commuters and has contacted triply in order to visualize and predict the potential impact of this change of schedule.
